# Best Loadouts for Every Operator in Rainbow Six Siege

## Overview

This document compiles the currently recommended "best" general‑purpose loadouts for every Rainbow Six Siege operator, split into attackers and defenders. The focus is on keyboard‑mouse ranked/competitive play but the setups are also usable on console with only minor sight or grip tweaks. Recommendations here are based on recent community and guide analysis of operator weapons, attachments, and gadget value in Year 11 season 1–2 era.[^1][^2][^3][^4]

Loadouts are presented in a compact table form per side, listing a primary, an alternative primary when relevant, a strong secondary, and the generally best default gadget for that operator. These are meant as "default meta" builds you can lock in without overthinking, then adapt per site, strat, or personal preference.[^1]

## Global Loadout Principles

- Attacker loadouts prioritize ARs and DMRs for mid‑to‑long range fights; these outperform LMGs and shotguns in most ranked scenarios when you are taking map control methodically.[^4][^1]
- Defender loadouts lean on SMGs, shotguns, and strong machine‑pistol secondaries to win close‑range and cross‑fire engagements around the site.[^4][^1]
- Magnified scopes (ACOG variants) on attackers are generally preferred when available; defenders typically run clean 1× optics to hold tight angles and swing close fights.[^1]
- Vertical grip is the default pick unless a gun clearly benefits from angled for faster ADS or a horizontal grip for recoil control on high‑kick DMRs.[^1]
- If a defender has a secondary shotgun or an SMG secondary, that option is almost always best for soft destruction and flexibility over a regular pistol.[^1]

## How to Adapt These Loadouts

- Map and site: On tight, close‑quarters maps or basement sites, consider swapping to shotguns or 1× optics even if a magnified scope is listed, to avoid tunnel vision and improve close‑range handling.[^1]
- Role in strat: Entry fraggers may prefer holo/1× on some rifles for faster ADS and easier tracking, while support players benefit more from magnified sights and stable DMR builds.[^1]
- Console vs PC: On console, horizontal recoil can feel worse, so a horizontal grip on DMRs and sometimes compensators on ARs can be more comfortable than vertical or flash hider in some cases.[^1]
- Personal comfort: If you shred with a non‑meta gun or a different sight, treat these tables as baselines, not hard rules; mechanical comfort is often more important than a small theoretical edge.[^4]

Using this document as a default reference lets you lock in strong, meta‑aligned loadouts for every operator, then iterate toward personal and team‑specific optimizations as you climb.[^3][^1]
